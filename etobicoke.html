WHAT WE DO IN ETOBICOKE

Built for Etobicoke's building stock.

Etobicoke is a patchwork — lakeshore condo towers, post-war bungalows, mid-century estates, and ravine-lot custom homes. Caspian works across all of them, with crews who've opened enough walls to know what they're looking at.

Basement waterproofing & sump pumps

Lower Etobicoke floods in spring — Alderwood, New Toronto, Mimico. We install sump pumps, backwater valves, and waterproofing systems that qualify for the City of Toronto rebate.

Humber Bay condo in-suite work

In-suite plumbing and HVAC for Humber Bay Shores, Mimico waterfront, and Islington-City Centre towers. Balcony drainage, fan-coil service, and condo board documentation.

Post-war bungalow retrofits

Original galvanized supply lines, cast-iron stack replacements, and lead service line swaps in Alderwood, New Toronto, and Long Branch. Brought up to current Toronto bylaw without ripping out more than we need to.

Boiler retrofits in mid-century homes

The Kingsway, Sunnylea, and Thorncrest Village have larger mechanical rooms and original radiant systems. We do boiler swaps, zone controls, and PEX in-floor heating without tearing up the slab.

Estate-home hydronics & premium fixtures

Multi-zone hydronics, snowmelt loops, and high-end fixture installs for Edenbridge-Humber Valley, Old Mill, and Princess Anne Manor. Comfortable working alongside architects and interior designers.

Commercial along The Queensway & Lake Shore

Grease traps, backflow, kitchen rough-in, and rooftop unit service for retail and restaurants along The Queensway, Lake Shore Blvd, and Bloor West. Familiar with TRCA permits near the rivers.

LOCAL EXPERTISE

Built for Etobicoke buildings.

Etobicoke plumbing isn't generic plumbing. We know what to expect before we open the wall.

Etobicoke was its own city until 1998 and it still wears that history in its building stock. The post-war bungalows of Alderwood, New Toronto, and Long Branch were built with galvanized supply, cast-iron stacks, and — in the older pockets — lead service lines still running to the City main. We've rebuilt enough of them to know what's coming before we cut the drywall.

Lower Etobicoke is flood country. Spring runoff from the Mimico and Etobicoke Creeks, combined with aging storm sewers, puts basements at risk from the lakeshore all the way up to Markland Wood. We install sump pumps, sewage ejectors, backwater valves, and waterproofing systems that qualify for the City of Toronto Basement Flooding Protection Subsidy — and we file the paperwork so you actually get the rebate.

The high-rise condo work in Humber Bay Shores, Mimico waterfront, and Islington-City Centre requires a different playbook. We coordinate with property management, schedule water shutoffs around concierge windows, protect common-element finishes, and produce documentation for condo boards. Balcony drainage, in-suite fan-coils, and risers are routine for us.

For The Kingsway, Princess Anne Manor, Thorncrest Village, and the estate homes of Edenbridge-Humber Valley and Old Mill, we work with larger mechanical rooms, multi-zone hydronics, and premium fixtures. Quick access from the 427, the Gardiner, and the QEW means our trucks get to your site fast — and we know the Etobicoke permit office on Eglinton Avenue West by name.
