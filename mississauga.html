LOCAL EXPERTISE

Built for Mississauga buildings.

Mississauga plumbing isn't generic plumbing. We know what to expect before we open the wall.

Mississauga's housing stock runs the full 70-year arc of postwar Ontario. A 1950s bungalow in Lakeview has original galvanized supply lines, a tired oil-converted boiler, and a basement that backs up every time the storm sewer overloads. Our techs have rebuilt enough of them to know what's coming before we cut the drywall — and we know the City of Mississauga's backwater-valve subsidy program inside out.

The Erin Mills, Meadowvale, and Streetsville subdivisions built in the 80s and 90s are now hitting the age where the original water heater, tub valves, and shutoffs all give up the same year. We see it every week. Tankless conversions, full PEX repipes, and pressure-balanced shower valve upgrades are the bread and butter of that whole corridor.

For Lorne Park, Sheridan, and Mineola custom builds we handle premium hydronics, multi-zone in-floor heating, large mechanical rooms, and the kind of detailed fixture install that lakefront budgets demand. For Square One and Hurontario towers we coordinate with property management, schedule water shutoffs around concierge windows, and produce documentation that condo boards will actually accept.

Mississauga logistics matter too. We work daily with the City of Mississauga building department and Peel Region water main inspectors — a different process than Toronto, and one our crews handle without learning curve. Quick dispatch across the 403, QEW, 410, and 401 keeps response times honest no matter where in the city the call comes from.
