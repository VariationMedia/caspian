WHAT WE DO IN NORTH YORK

Built for North York's building stock.

North York is bungalows, splits, estate homes, and the towers of the Yonge corridor — all built across different decades, all with their own quirks. Caspian works across the whole mix.

Post-war bungalow retrofits

Galvanized to copper or PEX, lead service replacement, knob-and-tube discoveries next to the furnace — typical Willowdale, Lansing, and Newtonbrook 1950s housing stock.

Mid-century mechanical upgrades

Original boilers, in-floor radiant heat, and aging copper in Bayview Village and Don Mills split-levels. Boiler swaps, zone controls, and heat pump conversions done cleanly.

Estate-home hydronics & multi-zone

Multi-zone hydronic systems, large mechanical rooms, and high-end fixtures across Hoggs Hollow, York Mills, and the Bridle Path-adjacent estates. Built for systems that have to perform year after year.

Yonge corridor condo mechanical

In-suite plumbing and HVAC for Yonge & Sheppard, Yonge & Eglinton, and North York Centre towers. Comfortable with concierge, building engineers, and condo board approval timelines.

Ravine-lot drainage & storm management

Sump pumps, backwater valves, and storm drainage for ravine-lot homes in Don Mills, Hoggs Hollow, and along the Don Valley — built for grades and runoff that catch other contractors out.

24/7 emergency on the Yonge corridor

Burst pipes, no-heat calls, suite floods — under-60-minute response along Yonge and the 401. Real plumbers, no after-hours surcharge.

LOCAL EXPERTISE

Built for North York buildings.

North York plumbing isn't generic plumbing. We know what to expect before we open the wall.

North York's housing stock is dominated by post-war detached homes — the 1950s and 60s bungalows of Willowdale, Lansing, and Newtonbrook, with original galvanized supply lines, oversized cast-iron stacks, and the occasional run of knob-and-tube still hiding beside a 50-year-old furnace. Our techs have rebuilt enough of them to know what's coming before the saw goes in.

The mid-century split-levels in Bayview Village and Don Mills bring their own set of challenges — original copper at end-of-life, radiant in-floor heating that still works but needs careful diagnosis, and custom fixtures that nobody stocks anymore. Estate homes in Hoggs Hollow, York Mills, and Lawrence Park add multi-zone hydronic systems, large mechanical rooms, and high-end finishes that demand documentation and protection.

North York sits on top of the former municipality's older infrastructure, which means lead service line replacement is a regular part of our work, especially in pre-1960 pockets. Ravine-lot drainage — common across Don Mills, Hoggs Hollow, and the streets that back onto the Don Valley — calls for sump pumps, backwater valves, and weeping tile work that accounts for grade and seasonal runoff.

For the high-rise corridor through Yonge & Sheppard, Yonge & Eglinton, and North York Centre we coordinate with property management, schedule water shutoffs around concierge windows, and produce paperwork suitable for condo board approval. We work familiarly with the North York permit office and inspectors, which keeps renovation timelines from stalling at the wrong moment.
