REPLACE VS REPAIR

Know when it's done.

Most water heaters give you warning signs before they fail. Catch them early and you can plan the replacement — miss them and you'll be mopping the basement.

01

Tank is 10+ years old

Standard residential tanks have an 8–12 year lifespan. Past year ten, you're on borrowed time — plan the replacement before you're forced into it.

02

Rust-coloured hot water

Brown or orange hot water (but clear cold water) means the steel tank is corroding from the inside. Once it's started, it doesn't stop.

03

Water pooling at the base

A puddle under the tank means the tank itself is leaking. Tank leaks are not repairable — only the surrounding fittings are. Replace it before it fails completely.

04

Repairs >50% of replacement cost

If we're quoting a repair over $800 on an aging tank, replacement is the smarter call. We'll give you both numbers, honestly, so you can decide.

HOW TO CHOOSE

Tankless vs tank — what we'd actually recommend.

The honest answer depends on your household. Here's how we'd advise you, based on the homes we install for every week across the GTA.

Traditional

Tank water heater

$1,500 – $3,000 installed
  • Lower upfront cost — fits most replacement budgets
  • 8–12 year typical service life
  • Simpler system, fewer components to fail
  • Works with existing venting in most homes
  • Standby heat loss — energy is used keeping water hot 24/7

Best for stable households of 2–4 with predictable daily hot water use.

High-efficiency

Tankless water heater

$3,500 – $5,500 installed
  • Higher upfront — gas line upsize and venting often required
  • 20+ year service life with annual descaling
  • Endless hot water — no recovery time between showers
  • Up to 40% lower operating cost — no standby loss
  • Wall-mounted — frees up basement floor space

Best for large families, long-term homeowners, and low-use vacation properties.
